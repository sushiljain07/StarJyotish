### D10 Chart — Key Placements

In the D10 chart, the 10th house contains: **Jupiter, Mercury, Saturn, Ketu**

This stellium in D10's 10th house strongly indicates:
- **Jupiter** → Law, justice, teaching, leadership
- **Mercury** → Communication, intellect, media influence
- **Saturn** → Service to the masses, discipline, persistence
- **Ketu** → Spiritual purpose, detachment, past-life karma

---

### Analysis

**Step 1 — Atmakaraka (Moon):**
Moon = nurturing, public, masses, emotional connection. Gandhi's soul purpose (Atmakaraka) was deeply connected to the people — their welfare, their emotions.

**Step 2 — Amatyakaraka (Jupiter):**
Jupiter = law, justice, teaching, moral leadership. The "minister" planet guiding career toward legal/advisory/leadership roles.

**Step 3 — AMK + AK Association:**
Moon and Jupiter are very close in degree (28°20' vs 28°08') — this near-conjunction is a **Raj Yoga of First Order**, indicating a person of immense public stature whose career serves a higher purpose.

**Step 4 — D10 10th House:**
Jupiter + Mercury + Saturn + Ketu in D10's 10th house confirms: legal career (Jupiter), intellectual/communicative influence (Mercury), service to masses through discipline and struggle (Saturn), and spiritual/karmic mission (Ketu).

**Conclusion:**
The chart perfectly explains Gandhi's career trajectory — starting as a Barrister (Jupiter/Mercury AMK), evolving into a mass political leader and spiritual icon (Moon AK + Saturn/Ketu in D10 10th house).

## How to Apply Case Study Method to Any Chart

When analyzing a chart, follow Gandhi's chart as a template:

1. **Find Atmakaraka** — highest degree planet → soul purpose
2. **Find Amatyakaraka** — second-highest degree planet → career direction
3. **Check D1 10th house** — which planets sit or aspect it?
4. **Check D10 10th house** — which planets are placed there?
5. **Look for AMK in D10** — its house placement and conjunctions define the career arena
6. **See if AK and AMK are associated** — if yes, Raj Yoga is present
7. **Cross-reference with running Dasha** — to understand timing of career peaks
