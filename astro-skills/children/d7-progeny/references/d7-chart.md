## What is the Saptamsha Chart?

The **D7 (Saptamsha)** is used **exclusively** for analyzing:

1. Possibility of childbirth
2. Number and health of children
3. Relationship with children
4. Progeny blessings
5. Karmic challenges related to children
6. Delays and obstacles in childbirth

Each zodiac sign is divided into **7 equal parts** (~4°17' each). The section where the D7 Lagna falls determines the native's fundamental quality of relationship with their children, linked to the ancient concept of **Sapt Ras** (Seven Essences).

---

## How to Analyse the D7 Chart

### Step 1: D7 Lagna
The **Lagna of D7** represents your overall destiny related to children.

- A **strong Lagna and Lagna lord** → good progeny fortune
- **Benefics in Lagna** (Jupiter, Venus, Mercury, Moon) → highly positive for childbirth
- **Malefics in/aspecting Lagna** (Saturn, Mars, Rahu, Ketu) → delays, struggles, or health issues in childbirth

### Step 2: Lagna Lord's Position
- **Well-placed** (Kendra or Trikona) → strong reproductive health
- **Debilitated, combust, or in 6th/8th/12th** → difficulties conceiving or carrying to full term

## The Sapt Ras Theory (Seven Essences)

Each zodiac sign in Saptamsha is divided into **7 equal sections of ~4°17'** each, named after the seven sacred essences used in Vedic ritual. These are linked to the **Seven Saptarishis** (Brahma's mind-born sages): Marichi, Atri, Angiras, Pulastya, Pulaha, Kratu, and Vashishtha.

### Division Table (Odd and Even Signs)

| Division | Degrees | Odd Sign | Even Sign | Planet |
|----------|---------|----------|-----------|--------|
| 1st | 0° – 4°17'08" | Kshara | Shuddha Jala | Saturn / Mars |
| 2nd | 4°17'08" – 8°34'17" | Ksheera | Madhya | Moon / Sun |
| 3rd | 8°34'17" – 12°51'25" | Dadhi | Ikshu | Venus / Mercury |
| 4th | 12°51'25" – 17°08'34" | Ajya | Ajya | Jupiter / Jupiter |
| 5th | 17°08'34" – 21°25'42" | Ikshu | Dadhi | Mercury / Venus |
| 6th | 21°25'42" – 25°42'51" | Madhya | Ksheera | Sun / Moon |
| 7th | 25°42'51" – 30° | Shuddha Jala | Kshara | Mars / Saturn |

> **Note:** For odd signs, the sequence goes Kshara → Ksheera → Dadhi → Ajya → Ikshu → Madhya → Shuddha Jala. For even signs it reverses: Shuddha Jala → Madhya → Ikshu → Ajya → Dadhi → Ksheera → Kshara.

## Qualities of Each Sapt Ras

### 1. Kshara (Salt / Alkali) — Saturn
- Represents **hardship**
- Native may experience stress, humiliation, or sorrow through children
- The soul finds room for transformation — either through ambition and boundless energy, or the noble qualities of wisdom and healing
- Depending on planetary influences: a path of struggle leading to strength, or one of cunning and ethical dilemmas

### 2. Ksheera (Milk) — Moon
- Signifies **purity, nourishment, and sattvic qualities**
- Children born under this division tend to uplift parents emotionally and socially
- Their inner nature often shines with dynamic energy, discipline, healing capabilities, and spiritual strength — provided surrounding influences remain favorable

### 3. Dadhi (Curd) — Venus
- Denotes **individuality and consistency**
- Children often carry a unique identity, bringing joy and pride
- Their expression may range from spiritual justice and inner strength to fierce intensity and a bold personality
- Emotional depth and grace are strong possibilities

### 4. Ajya / Ghritha (Ghee) — Jupiter
- Symbolizes **spiritual refinement and richness**
- Children are likely to be respectful, devout, and bring wealth or inner prosperity to the family
- Emotional grace and divine energy dominate, though there's sometimes a streak of restlessness and shifting interest

### 5. Ikshu / Ikshuras (Sugarcane Juice) — Mercury
- **Sweet and vibrant** in temperament; children are generally affectionate and strong
- Outwardly dynamic and inwardly soft; they can bring joy
- If overly indulged, may become self-focused or arrogant

### 6. Madhya / Madya (Wine) — Sun
- Reflects **indulgence, charm, and a touch of illusion**
- Such children may be lively and pleasure-seeking, sometimes neglecting responsibilities
- With spiritual inclination and creative passion, they can evolve into imaginative and thoughtful individuals

### 7. Shuddha Jala / Shuddhajal (Pure Water) — Mars
- Represents **clarity, innocence, and adaptability**
- Natives are sensitive and often exhibit spiritual promise from an early age
- If nurtured well: calm, wise, compassionate souls
- If misguided: purity may turn into confusion or misdirection
- Deep within lies patience, creative fertility, healing powers, and noble intentions

## How to Calculate the Sapt Ras Division (Step-by-Step)

**Example:** D1 Lagna at 6° Aquarius

1. **Step 1 — Odd or Even?** Aquarius is an odd sign → use the **linear order** (Kshara, Ksheera, Dadhi, Ajya, Ikshu, Madhya, Shuddha Jala)
2. **Step 2 — Which division?** Each division is ~4°17'. At 6°, this falls in the **2nd division** (4°17' – 8°34')
3. **Step 3 — Match the name:** In odd signs, 2nd division = **Ksheera (Milk)**

**Result:** D7 Lagna falls in Ksheera division → emotionally connected child, nurtures family bonds; children born under this division uplift parents emotionally and socially; wise soul, spiritually rich child, teacher lineage.
