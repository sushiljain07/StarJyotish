# Gems Do's and Don'ts

A comprehensive reference for practical rules when buying, wearing, and disposing of astrological gemstones.

## DO's

1. **You can remove and re-wear the gem temporarily** for physical tasks, sports, surgery, etc. Temporarily removing it does not break activation.
2. **After the stone reaches its prescribed age** — immerse in running water or bury in a plant pot. If it cracked before its age was up, place it in a drawer as décor.
3. **If a stone breaks, cracks, or gets a deep scratch — it is a good sign.** The stone absorbed a negative event on your behalf. Replace it immediately; do not continue wearing it.
4. **Small scratches are harmless.** Do not worry about minor surface scratches.
5. **Always check for a certificate of authenticity.** Certificates must come from trusted gemological laboratories.
6. **Expensive and less expensive stones of the same type have the same astrological effects.** Choose based on your budget.
7. **Wear only stones that suit your chart.** Do not wear a stone just because it is popular or someone else had good results.
8. **Give donations to the needy.** Never give donations (dana) to family members.
9. **Wear the ring on the right hand / writing/working hand** whenever possible.
10. **If you cannot afford gold, opt for panchdhatu (five-metal alloy).**
11. **Abhimantran (activation) is the most important step.** Without it, the stone is a mere mineral and gives no results.
12. **Recommended size: one-tenth of body weight in carats.** (E.g., 60 kg body weight → 6 carats.)
13. **Wearing substitutes on the left hand or non-working hand is acceptable**, especially for Opal.
14. **Cat's Eye needs a special recommendation** — recommend it only if the native cannot let go of the stone despite guidance.
15. **Rings of 1st, 5th, or 9th house lords can be worn** (trinal lords are generally considered auspicious for all lagnas).
16. **Children can wear 2–3 carat stones**, especially in a pendant rather than a ring.
17. **Always check astrologically before wearing any stone.** No stone is universally safe for everyone.
18. **Donations for planets with bad effects are encouraged for everyone**, even those not wearing any stone.
19. **Do seva** — serve others; this counteracts negative planetary karma more effectively than any gem alone.
20. **Wear the prescribed weight.** Small, decorative stones carry less energy and may give diminished or no results.

## DON'Ts

1. **Never wear second-hand or worn-out gems** that belonged to another person. Their energy is already programmed.
2. **Never buy from buy-back stores or schemes.** These are often used/returned stones.
3. **Never re-use cracked or broken stones.** Replace them.
4. **Avoid giving donations to family members** (it does not count as charitable dana in the Vedic sense).
5. **Do not chase 8 dattu / ashtadhatu** rings — true ashtadhatu (eight-metal alloy) is extremely rare. Most claims are false.
6. **Do not confuse units of weight:**
   - **KARAT** = gold purity (e.g., 22K gold)
   - **CARAT** = weight of a gemstone
   - **RATTI** = traditional Indian weight measure for stones (~0.91 carats)
7. **Avoid rings in steel or iron.** These metals are inauspicious for astrological rings.
8. **Do not wear 9 RATNA rings (Navratna rings).** Wearing all nine stones together is not recommended in contemporary Vedic practice — incompatible combinations cancel or harm each other.
9. **Do not wear more than 3 stones simultaneously.**
10. **Do not wear stones to cure anger or emotional imbalances.** Gems work on planetary energies, not psychological states.
11. **Avoid giving perfumes as a donation** (unless specified as Venus/Shukra remedy — even then, do not give to family members).
12. **Never exchange idols or deity statues with anyone.**
13. **Do not eat for free in bhandaras** (community feasts). If you attend, contribute.
