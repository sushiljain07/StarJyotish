# Procedure to Wear Gemstones, Beej Mantras, and Trial Methods

## The activation procedure

A gemstone worn as an astrological remedy must be ritually activated before first wear:

1. Choose **the planetary day of Shukla Paksha** (waxing moon fortnight) or the **hora (hour) of the ruling planet**.
2. **The evening before**, prepare **Panchamrit** (also written Panchamitra): mix equal quantities of **milk, sugar, gangajal/water, tulsi leaves, ghee, curd, and honey**.
3. Take a **small steel bowl** and submerge the ring in the Panchamrit overnight.
4. The **next morning**, after taking a bath, set up the **pooja thali** — diya, incense sticks, flowers, dhoop, etc.
5. **Sit facing North or North-East.**
6. Offer prayers following your usual daily rituals.
7. Complete the activation by chanting the **beej mantra of the planet 108 times.**
8. The gemstone is now activated (Abhimantrit) and ready to be worn.

> **Abhimantran is the single most important step.** Without proper activation, the stone is a mere mineral and gives no astrological results, regardless of quality or price.

## Trial methods for compatibility

Some gemstones (especially **Blue Sapphire** and **Cat's Eye**) can give sudden positive *or* negative results. A trial is required for these and is recommended for any stone the chart leaves uncertain. Three methods, all done over **3 consecutive days**:

1. **Arm-bind method.** Tie the stone in a small cloth of the matching colour. Wear it tied around the arm for 24 hours, repeating across 3 days.
2. **Pillow method.** Wrap the stone in the matching coloured cloth and keep it under the pillow for 3 consecutive nights. Check sleep and dream quality.
3. **Meditation method.** Hold the stone in the palm during meditation for 3 consecutive days.

Any of the following during a trial = **stop**: sudden bad dreams, accidents, conflict, sudden financial setbacks, illness, persistent unease.

> **Common myth about Blue Sapphire (Neelam):** It is a widespread misconception that Neelam causes cough, colds, accidents, or misfortune. This is **not true** — Neelam is a perfectly safe stone when the chart supports it and the Abhimantran is done properly. The 3-day trial is a precaution, not a signal that the stone is dangerous by nature.

> **Another myth:** Keeping Neelam under the pillow for 3 days is the "right" way to trial it. In fact, **any** of the three methods above is valid; the pillow method is just one option, not a special rule for Neelam.

## General notes (apply to all stones)

- **Solitaire only.** A gemstone worn as an astrological remedy must be a solitaire — small ornamental stones give no results.
- **Naturally sourced only.** Lab-grown gemstones do not work as astrological remedies.
- **Prescribed weight.** Wear the prescribed weight. Small stones carry less energy. Standard guideline: **1/10th of body weight in carats** (e.g., 70 kg body weight → 7 carat stone).
- **Replace at age of stone.** Each stone has a recommended active life (see individual stone files). After that age, immerse in running water or bury in a plant pot. Do not re-use cracked or broken stones.
- **If a stone cracks or breaks mid-wear — it is considered a good sign** (it absorbed a negative event). Replace it; do not continue wearing the broken stone.
- **Substitutes weigh more.** Substitute stones must weigh more in carats than what was recommended for the original.
- **Wear on the right/working hand** whenever possible.
- **Never wear more than 3 stones** simultaneously.
- **Donate for afflicted planets** even when not wearing a stone — donations are encouraged for everyone.

## Power hierarchy

Ratna (Navratna gems) > Substitute gemstones > Crystals

- **Astrology** leans toward gem/ratna recommendations.
- **Numerology** leans toward crystal recommendations.
- For crystals: raw stones > clusters/towers/geodes/pyramids (bulky) > bracelets/pendants (smaller).
- Crystals are applicable across all religions.

## Weights: terminology note

**KARAT** = gold purity. **CARAT** = weight of a stone. **RATTI** = traditional Indian weight of a stone (~0.91 carats). Do not confuse carat and ratti.
