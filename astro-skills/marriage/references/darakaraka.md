# Darakaraka — The Spouse Significator

Darakaraka is one of the most important concepts in Jaimini Astrology for understanding the spouse. It describes the *nature, appearance, profession, and approach to relationships* of the future or current spouse.

## The definition

> **Darakaraka** = the planet with the **lowest degree** in the D-1 (Lagna / Rashi) chart, excluding Rahu and Ketu.

The Sanskrit roots:
- **Dara** = spouse, partner, wife/husband
- **Karaka** = significator, indicator

Of the seven planets — Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn — whichever has wandered the *fewest* degrees into its current sign is the Darakaraka. Rahu and Ketu (the lunar nodes) are excluded because they are shadow planets, not physical bodies.

## How to calculate it — step by step

1. **List the degrees** of all seven planets from the D-1 chart. Use only the degrees within the sign (0°–30°), not the absolute longitude.
2. **Drop Rahu and Ketu** from the list.
3. **Identify the smallest degree.** That planet is the Darakaraka.

### Worked example 1 — generic chart

Given degrees:
- Sun 18°
- Moon 29°
- Mars 19°
- Mercury 22°
- Jupiter 23°
- Venus 21°
- **Saturn 9°** ← smallest
- Rahu 17° (excluded)
- Ketu 17° (excluded)

→ **Darakaraka = Saturn (9°)**.

### Worked example 2 — Shah Rukh Khan's chart

Given degrees:
- Sun 15°, Moon 21°, Mars 27°, Mercury 6°, Jupiter 7°, **Venus 2°**, Saturn 17°

→ **Darakaraka = Venus (2°)** — consistent with his public persona around love, romance, and creative arts as a film actor.

### Worked example 3 — Madhuri Dixit's chart

Given degrees:
- **Sun 0°**, Moon 9°, Mars 22°, Mercury 5°, Jupiter 6°, Venus 12°, Saturn 15°

→ **Darakaraka = Sun (0°)** — pointing to a confident, authoritative, possibly self-employed/leader spouse.

### Worked example 4 — Deepika Padukone's chart

Given degrees:
- Sun 20°, Moon 6°, Mars 19°, **Mercury 5°**, Jupiter 25°, Venus 17°, Saturn 11°

→ **Darakaraka = Mercury (5°)** — pointing to a young, communicative, intellectually inclined spouse who works with words/media/business.

## Tie-breaking and edge cases

- **Two planets at the same degree** is extremely rare with full minute-second precision; in practice, drop to minutes (and seconds if needed) to break the tie. The lower one wins.
- **Combust planets** (planets too close to the Sun) still count — combustion does not exclude a planet from Darakaraka calculation.
- **Retrograde planets** still count — retrogression does not exclude a planet either.
- **Atmakaraka conflict**: in stricter Jaimini schools, if the Atmakaraka and Darakaraka would be the same planet, the second-lowest is taken as Darakaraka. The Day 1 material does not invoke this rule, but be aware of it if a sophisticated user asks.

## What each Darakaraka indicates about the spouse

These are read alongside the 7th house sign and the 7th lord — the Darakaraka colors the description but doesn't replace the other indicators.

### Sun as Darakaraka
- Spouse is confident, authoritative, charismatic, with strong personality
- Often older or more senior in age, status, or position
- May be involved in government, politics, leadership, or self-employed
- Healthy ego — relationship needs space for the spouse's identity to shine
- Father figure energy in the marriage

### Moon as Darakaraka
- Spouse is emotional, nurturing, sensitive, intuitive, family-oriented
- Often beautiful, fair-skinned, soft-spoken
- Strong attachment to mother / family of origin
- May have moodiness or emotional fluctuations
- Caring and protective, "homely" type partner

### Mars as Darakaraka
- Spouse is energetic, athletic, courageous, sometimes aggressive
- Strong-willed, action-oriented, possibly in defense, sports, surgery, or engineering
- Can have a temper; relationship may have intensity and conflict
- Physical attraction is strong; the marriage has heat
- Often a partner with leadership / commanding nature

### Mercury as Darakaraka
- Spouse is youthful (or appears younger), intellectual, communicative
- Works in writing, media, IT, business, finance, teaching, or analytics
- Witty, talkative, sometimes restless
- Often the spouse stays youthful / boyish or girlish across life
- Marriage is conversational, idea-driven, mentally stimulating

### Jupiter as Darakaraka
- Spouse is wise, knowledgeable, religious, ethical, generous
- Often a teacher, lawyer, advisor, judge, or in education/finance/spiritual work
- Marriage brings wisdom, prosperity, and dharmic growth
- Spouse may be older, fatherly, or guru-like
- Considered one of the most blessed Darakarakas

### Venus as Darakaraka
- Spouse is beautiful, charming, refined, artistic, sensual
- Loves luxury, fine clothes, fragrance, music, art
- Works in arts, fashion, design, beauty, hospitality, entertainment
- Marriage is romantic and pleasure-focused
- Considered the *natural* significator of spouse, so Venus as Darakaraka is generally favourable

### Saturn as Darakaraka
- Spouse is older, mature, disciplined, hardworking, reliable
- Late marriage is common; the relationship takes time to form
- Spouse often comes from a humble background or works in service-oriented, traditional, structured fields
- Long-lasting and serious bond once formed
- Initial coldness can mature into deep loyalty

## How to use Darakaraka with the 7th house

A complete spouse-description analysis layers three things:

1. **The sign in the 7th house** — gives the broad personality (Libra → diplomatic; Capricorn → disciplined; etc., from `basics.md`).
2. **The 7th lord's house and sign** — refines circumstances of meeting and the spouse's environment.
3. **The Darakaraka planet's nature** — gives the deepest psychological texture of the spouse.

When all three line up (e.g., Venus in 7th, Libra in 7th, Venus as Darakaraka) the description is unambiguous. When they conflict (e.g., Mars in 7th but Saturn as Darakaraka), present both and explain that the spouse will have a complex personality combining the two energies.

## Darakaraka in timing — Technique 19

The Darakaraka also features in marriage timing. **Technique 19** in `timing-techniques.md` says: marriage triggers when transiting Jupiter aspects or sits with the Darakaraka in the natal chart. So the lowest-degree planet matters not just for *who* but also for *when*.
