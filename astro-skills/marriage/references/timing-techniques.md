This is the workhorse file for any "when will I get married" question. It contains all 23 techniques from the source material, each with its formula, logic, and a worked case study. Use these in *combination*, not in isolation — predictions become reliable when 2–3 techniques converge on the same window.

## How to use this file

Almost every question reduces to: **which dasha + which Jupiter transit?** Group A (dasha) tells you which mahadasha, antardasha, pratyantar dasha. Group B (longitudes + Jupiter) tells you which year. Group C (Saturn, Mars, Venus) narrows to month. Technique 23 is a one-shot age estimator.

A good prediction cites 3–5 techniques that all point to the same window. If only one technique fires, soften the prediction.

## Group A — Dasha-based techniques (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 12, 14, 15)

These tell you *which planetary period* will deliver the marriage.

### Technique 1 — Dasha of the lord of the sign where 7th HL of D-1 sits

**Rule:** Marriage may occur during the dasha (Mahadasha or Antardasha) of the lord of the sign where the 7th house lord (D-1 chart) is placed.

**Steps:**
1. Identify the 7th house lord of the D-1 chart.
2. Find the sign that 7th HL is placed in.
3. Find the lord of that sign.
4. Marriage triggers in that planet's dasha or sub-dasha.

### Technique 2 — Venus's own period

**Rule:** The position of Venus can give results of marriage during Venus's own dasha or antardasha — Venus is the natural significator of marriage.

**Note:** This is one of the most common triggers. Almost every marriage chart has Venus's antardasha somewhere in the run-up to the wedding. If a Venus dasha or bhukti is running near a likely window, it strongly reinforces the prediction.

### Technique 3 — Dasha of the lord of the sign where 7th HL of D-9 sits

**Rule:** Same logic as Technique 1, but using the D-9 (Navamsa) chart.

**Steps:**
1. Identify the 7th house lord of the D-9 chart.
2. Find the sign that D-9 7th HL is placed in.
3. Find the lord of that sign.
4. Marriage triggers in that planet's dasha or sub-dasha.

### Technique 4 — 7th lord's own dasha (associated with Venus)

**Rule:** The lord of the 7th house in its own Dasha-Bhukti can bring marriage, *provided it is associated with Venus* — by conjunction, by aspect, by being in Venus's sign, or by being in mutual reception with Venus.

**Worked case study (combined Techniques 1, 3, 4 + transit):**

> 7th Lord of D-1 = Venus.
> 7th Lord of D-9 = Mars.
> Dasha during marriage: Ve → Me → Su → Ma.
> Jupiter Transit: Cancer (4 July 2002 → 29 July 2003).
> Date of marriage: **20 January 2003**. ✓
>
> Reading: The Venus mahadasha (Tech 2 + Tech 4) was running. Mercury was the antardasha; Sun the pratyantar dasha; Mars (D-9 7th lord, Tech 3) the sookshma dasha. Multiple techniques converged.

**Another case study:**

> 7th Lord of D-1 = Jupiter.
> 7th Lord of D-9 = Venus.
> Dasha during marriage: Ra → Ve → Ju → Me.
> Jupiter Transit: Virgo (11 Sept 1992 → 12 Dec 1993).
> Date of marriage: **30 November 1992**. ✓
>
> Reading: Venus antardasha (Tech 2) + Jupiter pratyantar (Tech 4 — Jupiter is the 7th lord) + Mercury sookshma. Three layers of dasha pointing at the 7th lord's planets.

### Technique 5 — 2nd house lord's dasha

**Rule:** Marriage can take place during the period of the 2nd house lord. The 2nd house signifies family (which expands at marriage), so the 2nd lord's dasha is a marriage trigger too.

### Technique 6 — Lord of the sign occupied by the 2nd HL

**Rule:** When the dasha-bhukti of the lord of the sign occupied by the 2nd HL comes, marriage may result.

**Worked case study (Tech 5 + Tech 6):**

> 2nd HL of D-1 = Moon.
> Moon is placed in the 11th house in Aries (lord = Mars).
> Dasha during Marriage: Mo → Ju → Ma → Ve.
> Date of Marriage: **6 November 2016**. ✓
>
> Reading: Moon mahadasha (Tech 5 — Moon is the 2nd lord). Mars pratyantar (Tech 6 — Mars is the lord of Moon's sign). Venus sookshma (Tech 2 — natural karaka). Three techniques stacking.

### Technique 7 — Lord of the sign occupied by the 2nd HL in D-9

**Rule:** Same as Technique 6 but applied to the D-9 chart.

**Worked case study:**

> 2nd HL in D-9 chart = Sun.
> Sun is placed in the 1st house: Cancer (lord = Moon).
> Dasha during marriage: Ju → Mo → Sat → Mo.
> Date of marriage: **13 May 2019**. ✓
>
> Reading: The 2nd house in the D-9 ties directly into family. The Moon mahadasha plus repeated Moon sub-periods (Tech 7 firing twice) brought the marriage event.

### Technique 12 — Strong planets connected to the 7th lord

**Rule:** When the dasha-bhukti of strong planets connected to the 7th lord (in either the D-1 or D-9) comes, it can indicate marriage. Reinforced when transit Jupiter moves over or trines the sign where the 7th lord sits in the D-1 or D-9.

**Worked case study:**

> Dasha during marriage: Su → Su → Ju → Ra.
> Transit Jupiter: Leo (13 July 2015 → 11 August 2016).
> Date of marriage: **31 October 2015**. ✓
>
> Reading: Sun mahadasha + Sun antardasha (Sun being a strong planet connected to the 7th lord in this chart) + Jupiter pratyantar dasha — and Jupiter transiting Leo (a key marriage rashi for this chart) at the same time. Layered triggers.

### Technique 14 — Dasha-Bhukti of planets aspecting the 7th house

**Rule:** Marriage can occur in the dasha or bhukti of any planet that aspects the 7th house in the natal chart. Use the special aspects of Mars (4/7/8), Jupiter (5/7/9), and Saturn (3/7/10) plus the standard 7th aspect of all planets.

**Worked case study:**

> Aspects on 7th House: Saturn, Sun, Jupiter (three planets aspecting the 7th).
> Dasha during marriage: Mo → Ju → Su → Mo.
> Date of marriage: **3 May 2009**. ✓
>
> Reading: Jupiter antardasha (Jupiter aspects 7th — Tech 14) + Sun pratyantar (Sun aspects 7th — Tech 14). Two of the three aspecting planets running their sub-periods at marriage time.

### Technique 15 — Trine of 7th lord's sign and Navamsa

**Rule:** During the dasha period of the planet causing marriage, the marriage will come in evidence when Jupiter transits in the trine (1st, 5th, or 9th from) of *both* the sign of the 7th lord in the D-1 and in the D-9.

**Worked case study:**

> 7th House Lord = Mars.
> Mars in D-1 is in Aquarius; in D-9 is in Libra.
> Dasha during marriage: Ju → Ju → Ju → Me.
> Transit Jupiter: Libra (11 Sept 2017 → 11 Dec 2018).
> Date of marriage: **12 August 2018**. ✓
>
> Reading: Libra is the 9th from Aquarius (trine in D-1 to Mars's sign) AND it *is* Mars's D-9 sign. So Jupiter in Libra was simultaneously in trine to Mars-in-D-1 and exactly on Mars-in-D-9. Massive trigger. The dasha was Jupiter's own — making the trigger doubly potent.

## Group B — Longitude + Jupiter transit techniques (8, 9, 10, 11, 17, 18, 19, 20)

These compute a **resultant rashi** by adding planetary longitudes. When Jupiter transits over that rashi (or its trine — 5th or 9th from it), marriage triggers. These convert "the right dasha" into "the right year".

The longitude formula is in `basics.md` — recap: longitude = (sign-number − 1) × 30 + degrees within sign. So 25° Sagittarius = 240 + 25 = 265°.

### Technique 8 — Lagna lord + 7th lord longitudes

**Rule:** Add the longitudes of the Lagna lord and the 7th lord. When Jupiter transits the resultant rashi (or its trines), marriage takes place.

**Steps:**
1. Find the Lagna lord and its longitude.
2. Find the 7th lord and its longitude.
3. Add. If the sum exceeds 360°, subtract 360° — *or use the formula 360 − (L1 + L2)* as in the source method.
4. Map the resultant degree back to a rashi using the longitude table.
5. Watch when Jupiter transits that rashi or its 5th / 9th house.

**Worked case study:**

> 1st HL = Jupiter at 25°25' Sagittarius. Longitude = 240 + 25 = 265°.
> 7th HL = Mercury at 6°57' Sagittarius. Longitude = 240 + 6 = 246°.
> Resultant: 360 − (265 + 246) = 360 − 511 = −151. Take absolute value or wrap → 151°, which is **Virgo** (150°–180°).
> Transit Jupiter during marriage: Capricorn (19 Nov 2020 → 5 April 2021), which is the 5th house from Virgo (a trine). ✓
> Date of Marriage: **30 November 2020**.
>
> Reading: Even though Jupiter wasn't sitting in Virgo itself, it was in Virgo's trine — the technique fires for both the rashi and its trines.

### Technique 9 — Moon + 7th lord-from-Moon longitudes

**Rule:** Add the longitude of the Moon and the longitude of the 7th house lord *as counted from the Moon*. When Jupiter transits the resultant rashi or its trines, marriage occurs.

**Worked case study:**

> Moon at 26°19' in Aquarius. Longitude = 300 + 26 = 326°.
> 7th from Moon = Leo. Sun rules Leo. Sun at 1°24' Sagittarius. Longitude = 240 + 1 = 241°.
> Resultant: 360 − (326 + 241) = 360 − 567 = −207. Wrap to 207°, which is **Libra** (180°–210°).
> Transit Jupiter during marriage: Gemini (30 May 2013 → 18 June 2014), the 9th from Libra (trine). ✓
> Date of Marriage: **19 May 2014**.

### Technique 10 — Moon + 5th lord + 9th lord (from Moon) longitudes

**Rule:** Add longitudes of the Moon, the 5th lord-from-Moon, and the 9th lord-from-Moon. Marriage triggers when Jupiter transits the resultant rashi or trine.

**Worked case study:**

> Moon at 14°45' in Aries. Longitude = 0 + 14 = 14°.
> 5th from Moon = Leo. Sun rules Leo. Sun at 26°29' Scorpio. Longitude = 210 + 26 = 236°.
> 9th from Moon = Sagittarius. Jupiter rules Sagittarius. Jupiter at 21°14' Aquarius. Longitude = 300 + 21 = 321°.
> Resultant: 360 − (14 + 236 + 321) = 360 − 571 = −211. Wrap to 211°, which is **Scorpio** (210°–230°).
> Transit Jupiter during marriage: Cancer (18 June 2014 → 13 July 2015), the 9th from Scorpio (trine). ✓
> Date of Marriage: **15 February 2015**.

### Technique 11 — Moon + 8th HL longitudes

**Rule:** Add longitudes of the Moon and the 8th house lord. Marriage occurs when transit Jupiter crosses that rashi.

**Worked case study:**

> Moon at 16°50' in Aquarius. Longitude = 300 + 16 = 316°.
> 8th HL = Saturn (8th house = Capricorn, Saturn rules Capricorn). Saturn at 17°44' Aquarius. Longitude = 300 + 17 = 317°.
> Resultant: 360 − (316 + 317) = 360 − 633 = −273. Wrap to 273°, which is **Capricorn** (270°–300°).
> Transit Jupiter during marriage: Capricorn (19 Nov 2020 → 5 April 2021). ✓
> Date of Marriage: **30 November 2020**.

### Technique 17 — Jupiter on the 5th house or 5th lord

**Rule:** When transiting Jupiter aspects or sits in the 5th house of the natal chart, *or* aspects / sits with the 5th house lord, it triggers marriage. (The 5th is the house of love and romance.)

**Worked case study:**

> 5th House = Libra. 5th HL = Venus.
> Transit Jupiter: Libra (11 Sept 2017 → 11 Dec 2018) — sitting in the 5th house and on the 5th lord's sign. ✓
> Date of marriage: **14 October 2018**.

### Technique 18 — Jupiter on the 9th house or 9th lord (rare)

**Rule:** In rare cases, when transit Jupiter aspects or sits in the 9th house, or aspects / sits with the 9th lord, marriage triggers. The 9th is the house of fortune and dharma.

**Worked case study:**

> Transit Jupiter: Aquarius (20 Nov 2021 → 13 April 2022) — sitting with the 9th lord. ✓
> Date of marriage: **9 February 2022**.

### Technique 19 — Jupiter on the Darakaraka

**Rule:** When transit Jupiter aspects or is placed with the Darakaraka in the natal chart, marriage triggers.

**Worked case study:**

> Darakaraka = Saturn at 4°51', placed in 8th house (Libra).
> Transit Jupiter: Aquarius (1 May 2009 → 30 July 2009). Jupiter in Aquarius gives a 9th aspect to Libra (Saturn's house). ✓
> Date of marriage: **5 May 2009**.

### Technique 20 — Jupiter on the 11th house or 11th lord (rare)

## Group C — Saturn, Mars, Venus refinements (13, 16, 21, 22)

These narrow the prediction window from *year* to *month* and confirm the dasha selection.

### Technique 13 — 7th lord or Venus through the Lagna lord's D-9 sign

**Rule:** When the 7th house lord *or* Venus moves through the sign in the Navamsa (D-9) chart where the Lagna lord is placed, or moves through the 5th or 9th sign (trines) from there, it indicates a favourable time for marriage.

**Worked case study:**

> 7th HL = Venus. Lagna Lord = Mars.
> Mars in D-9 is in Gemini. So Venus needs to transit Gemini (or its trines).
> Transit Venus: Gemini (2 May 2015 → 30 May 2015). ✓
> Date of Marriage: **12 May 2015**.

### Technique 16 — Stronger of Lagna lord and 7th lord → its Navamsa lord transits its own sign

**Rule:** Determine which is stronger between the Lagna lord and the 7th lord. Find that planet's Navamsa sign. Find the lord of that Navamsa sign. When that Navamsa-lord transits its own sign, marriage takes place.

**Worked case study:**

> Stronger Lord = Saturn (in Capricorn — own sign).
> Saturn is placed in Taurus in the Navamsa.
> Lord of Taurus = Venus.
> Transit Venus: Taurus (4 May 2021 → 29 May 2021). ✓
> Date of marriage: **21 May 2021**.

### Technique 21 — Saturn + Jupiter aspecting the 1/7 axis

**Rule:** The transit of Saturn and Jupiter and their aspect on the 7th house is the primary indication of marriage. Classical view: *"It is Saturn who unites the bride and bridegroom. Saturn in consultation with Jupiter proposes how and when to tie the conjugal knot."*

For the timing of the event, **Saturn** (and analogously Jupiter) should have an aspect or association with one of:

- Aspect on the Lagna *and* the 7th house
- Aspect on the Lagna lord *and* the 7th lord
- Aspect on the Lagna lord *and* the 7th house
- Aspect on the Lagna *and* the 7th lord

The same principle applies to Jupiter. Both planets working the 1/7 axis simultaneously is the *primary* timing indicator in classical thought.

**Worked case study:**

> 1st HL = Venus. 7th HL = Mars.
> Transit Jupiter: Libra (11 Sept 2017 → 11 Dec 2018). Jupiter's 5th aspect from Libra → Mars's house. Jupiter's 9th aspect from Libra → Venus's house. ✓
> Transit Saturn: Sagittarius (26 Jan 2017 → 23 Jan 2020). Saturn's 3rd aspect from Sagittarius → reaches Mars. Saturn's 7th aspect from Sagittarius → reaches Venus. ✓
> Date of marriage: **12 August 2018**.
>
> Reading: Both Saturn and Jupiter were simultaneously aspecting both the lagna lord and the 7th lord — the strongest possible Saturn–Jupiter signal.

### Technique 22 — Mars transit (narrowing to the month)

**Rule:** The position of transiting Mars narrows the *month* of marriage within the Saturn-Jupiter window. Mars must satisfy:

**Within the Saturn–Jupiter combined window**, Mars must cover the 1/7 axis within 6 months of marriage:
- Mars aspects the Lagna *and* the 7th house, OR
- Mars aspects the Lagna *and* the 7th lord, OR
- Mars aspects the Lagna lord *and* the 7th house, OR
- Mars aspects the Lagna lord *and* the 7th lord.

**Within the Jupiter window alone**, Mars should cover one of within 6 months:
- Venus and the 5th house
- Venus and the 5th lord
- The 5th house and the 5th lord

**Worked case study:**

> Transit Jupiter: Libra (11 Sept 2017 → 11 Dec 2018).
> Transit Saturn: Sagittarius (26 Oct 2017 → 23 Jan 2020).
> Transit Mars: Capricorn (2 May 2018 → 6 Nov 2018) — aspects (8th) reaches the 1/7 axis of this chart. ✓
> Date of marriage: **14 October 2018**.

## Standalone — Technique 23 (Age of marriage)

**Rule:** A rough age-of-marriage estimator from the 7th house sign alone.

**Steps:**
1. Find the sign in the 7th house. Note its number (Aries=1, Taurus=2, …, Pisces=12).
2. Add 8 to that number.
3. Add multiples of 5 (5, 10, 15, 20, …) to the result. The native may marry around any of those candidate ages.

**Generic example:** Leo Lagna → Aquarius (sign 11) in the 7th house. 11 + 8 = 19. Candidates: 19+5=24, 19+10=29, 19+15=34, 19+20=39. So the native is likely to marry around 24, 29, or 34.

**Real case study:**

> 7th House Rashi = Capricorn (sign 10).
> 10 + 8 = 18. Candidates: 23, 28, 33, 38.
> Date of Birth: 7 October 1988.
> Date of Marriage: 21 May 2021. Age at marriage: **33**. ✓
>
> Reading: The technique fired exactly on the 33-year candidate.

**Caveat:** This is a *rough* estimator. Use it for a sanity check on the more rigorous Group A + Group B + Group C analysis. If the rigorous techniques converge on a window that matches one of the candidate ages from Technique 23, confidence is very high.

## Putting it all together — a recommended workflow

When asked "when will I marry":

1. **Compute the seven core indicators** (from the main SKILL.md) — 7th lord D-1, 7th lord D-9, Venus, Lagna lord, Darakaraka, current dasha, current Jupiter transit position.
2. **Run all Group A techniques** on the user's chart and note which planet's dasha-bhukti the techniques point to.
3. **Run all Group B techniques** to identify the rashi where Jupiter must transit. Look up when Jupiter will *next* be in that rashi or its trines.
4. **Cross-reference**: when does the Group A planet's dasha-bhukti overlap with the Group B Jupiter transit? That overlap is your *primary window*.
5. **Apply Group C** — confirm with Saturn (Tech 21), then narrow to the month with Mars (Tech 22).
6. **Sanity check with Tech 23** — does the predicted age fall on or near a Tech 23 candidate?
7. **Report the window** with confidence calibrated to how many techniques converged. 5+ techniques converging = very high confidence; 2 techniques = moderate; 1 = a hint, not a prediction.

Always show the user which techniques fired and which didn't. Transparency is part of being useful.
